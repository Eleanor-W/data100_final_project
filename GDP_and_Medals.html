GDP Per Capita

An Overview of GDP per Capita

What is GDP per Capita?

Gross Domestic Product per Capita, referred to simply GDP per Capita, is a measure of economic output. GDP per capita provides a rough estimate of total amount of goods and services per person in a given country. The World Bank calculates GDP per capita by dividing Real GDP by mid-year population size. Real GDP is the total of all goods and services produced by the country, then adjusted for inflation.

Why does it matter?

Aside from obvious economic usages, GDP per Capita is often used to evaluate the standard of living in a country. In the past, the United Nations considered GDP per capita when determing Human Development Index(HDI) values for the countries of the world. Since 2010, however, the UN has used different economic measures, but GDP per capita still remains an accurate and prevalent measure of development. A high GDP per capita typically indicates a high level of standard of living in a country, with many of the countries with the highest GDP per capitas also having some of the highest rankings on the HDI.
